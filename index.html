Who we are

Energy access shouldn't wait for the grid.

Founded in 2018 by a group of engineers and community organisers in the Western Region, SunRoot Foundation partners with fishing towns, farming communities, and schools that sit far from Ghana's national electricity grid. We install solar systems people can maintain themselves, train local technicians, and hand over ownership — not just equipment.

We believe the fastest path to sustainable development runs through community-owned infrastructure, not donated hardware that breaks down once the NGO leaves.

More About Our Story

Did you know?

  • Over 30% of households in Ghana's rural Western Region still rely on kerosene lamps or torchlights after dark.
  • A single 5-panel microgrid can power a 40-household community for 25+ years.
  • Every SunRoot site trains at least 2 local technicians before we scale back involvement.
