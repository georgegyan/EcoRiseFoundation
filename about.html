Eight years of building energy access, together.

The story, principles, and people behind SunRoot Foundation.

Our history

From one broken streetlight to 38 communities

SunRoot Foundation began in 2018 when a small group of engineering graduates from the Western Region returned to their home town of Anlo Beach to find it still without reliable electricity, 61 years after independence. What started as a single solar-powered streetlight, funded from personal savings, grew into a full community microgrid within a year.

Word spread along the coast. By 2020 we had registered as a formal NGO and expanded into clean cookstoves and school electrification. Today, SunRoot works across 38 communities in the Western and Western North Regions, with a growing waiting list of towns asking to partner with us next.

We remain proudly community-first: every project is co-designed with the people who will maintain it long after our engineers have moved to the next site.

Why we exist

  • Grid expansion in rural coastal Ghana is slow and often bypasses smaller fishing and farming towns.
  • Off-grid solar is now cheaper than waiting years for grid connection.
  • Communities that own and maintain their own systems keep the lights on long-term.

What drives us

Vision, mission & the values behind every install

Vision

A Ghanaian coast fully lit by clean power

A future where no coastal or rural community in Ghana waits for the national grid to access safe, reliable, renewable energy.

Mission

Build it with, not for, communities

We design, install, and hand over solar and clean-energy infrastructure that communities own, operate, and maintain themselves.

Approach

Train technicians before we leave

Every SunRoot project includes a local technician training track, so skills — not just hardware — stay behind after we scale back.

Community Ownership

Projects belong to the people who use them, not to SunRoot.

Transparency

Every donation and its use is reported back to donors and communities alike.

Durability

We install for a 25-year lifespan, not a photo opportunity.

Local Skill

Hiring and training local technicians is never optional.

Our objectives

What we're working toward by 2030

  • 01 · Reach 75 communities

    Nearly double our current footprint across the Western and Western North Regions.

  • 02 · Train 500 technicians

    Build a regional workforce capable of maintaining every system we install.

  • 03 · Electrify 100 schools

    Extend our School Solar Lab Program to every off-grid school in our operating area.

  • 04 · Cut 5,000 tonnes of CO₂

    Through solar displacement of diesel generators and kerosene lighting.
